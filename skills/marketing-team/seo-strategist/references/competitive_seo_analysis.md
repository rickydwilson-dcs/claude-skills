# Competitive SEO Analysis Framework

Systematic approach to analyzing competitor SEO strategies, identifying opportunities, and developing competitive positioning.

## Competitor Identification

### Types of Competitors

**Direct Competitors:**
- Same products/services
- Same target market
- Compete for same customers

**SERP Competitors:**
- Rank for your target keywords
- May not be direct business competitors
- Include informational sites, aggregators

**Aspirational Competitors:**
- Industry leaders
- Benchmark for best practices
- May be out of current league

### Identification Methods

1. **Keyword-based:** Search target keywords, note top 10 results
2. **Tool-based:** Use SEMrush/Ahrefs competitor discovery
3. **Industry knowledge:** Known players in your space
4. **Customer input:** Ask who else they considered

### Competitor Selection Criteria
- Select 3-5 primary competitors
- Mix of direct and SERP competitors
- Include 1 aspirational competitor
- Ensure data availability

## SERP Analysis Framework

### Position Tracking

**Data to Collect:**
| Keyword | Your Position | Comp A | Comp B | Comp C |
|---------|--------------|--------|--------|--------|
| [keyword] | #12 | #1 | #3 | #5 |

**Analysis Points:**
- Position distribution (top 3, top 10, top 50)
- Position changes over time
- Keywords where competitors outrank you
- Keywords where you outrank competitors

### SERP Feature Analysis

**Features to Track:**
- Featured snippets (who owns them?)
- People Also Ask (who appears?)
- Knowledge panels
- Local pack results
- Video carousels
- Image packs
- Shopping results

**Opportunity Identification:**
- Features no competitor owns
- Features with weak current holders
- Features aligned with your content strength

### Search Intent Match

For each target keyword:
1. Analyze top 3 results
2. Identify dominant content type
3. Note content format (list, guide, tool)
4. Assess search intent alignment

## Content Gap Analysis

### Process

1. **Export competitor content:**
   - Sitemap analysis
   - Crawl competitor site
   - Use Ahrefs/SEMrush content explorer

2. **Map competitor topics:**
   - Categorize by topic/theme
   - Note content types (blog, guide, tool)
   - Estimate traffic/value

3. **Compare to your content:**
   - Topics they cover, you don't
   - Topics you both cover (quality comparison)
   - Topics only you cover (differentiation)

### Gap Categories

**Topic Gaps:**
- Entire topics you haven't addressed
- Priority: High if aligned with business

**Depth Gaps:**
- Topics you cover superficially
- Competitor has more comprehensive content
- Priority: Medium-high

**Format Gaps:**
- Different content types (video, tools, templates)
- Interactive content
- Priority: Medium

**Freshness Gaps:**
- Outdated content vs. competitor's fresh content
- Priority: Varies by topic

### Gap Prioritization Matrix

| Gap Type | Business Value | Effort | Priority |
|----------|---------------|--------|----------|
| High-value topic gap | High | Medium | P1 |
| Depth gap on money page | High | High | P1 |
| Format gap (tool/calculator) | Medium | High | P2 |
| Low-competition topic gap | Medium | Low | P2 |
| Informational topic gap | Low | Low | P3 |

## Backlink Profile Comparison

### Metrics to Compare

| Metric | Your Site | Comp A | Comp B | Comp C |
|--------|-----------|--------|--------|--------|
| Domain Rating | 45 | 62 | 55 | 48 |
| Referring Domains | 250 | 890 | 520 | 310 |
| Backlinks | 1.2K | 5.6K | 3.1K | 1.8K |
| Referring IPs | 180 | 650 | 380 | 220 |

### Link Gap Analysis

**Find links competitors have that you don't:**
1. Export competitor backlinks
2. Export your backlinks
3. Compare and find gaps
4. Prioritize by authority/relevance

**Link Opportunity Types:**
- Resource pages linking to competitors
- Broken links (replace competitor's broken links)
- Guest post opportunities
- Industry directories
- Partner/vendor links

### Link Quality Assessment

**Good links:**
- High DR/DA sites
- Topically relevant
- Editorial placement
- Diverse anchor text

**Suspicious patterns:**
- Sudden link spikes
- Low-quality directories
- Exact match anchor clusters
- PBN patterns

## Keyword Overlap Analysis

### Overlap Categories

```
         Your Keywords
        /              \
   Unique            Shared
   to You          /        \
                 You       Shared
                Lead      Competitor
                          Leads
                               \
                              Unique to
                              Competitor
```

### Strategic Implications

**Unique to You:**
- Competitive advantage
- Defend and expand
- May indicate missed opportunities for competitors

**You Lead:**
- Protect rankings
- Optimize for featured snippets
- Expand to related keywords

**Competitor Leads:**
- Analyze why (better content? More links?)
- Create competitive content
- Target long-tail variants

**Unique to Competitor:**
- Potential opportunities
- Validate against business goals
- Prioritize by value

## Competitive Positioning Strategy

### Position Options

**Head-to-Head:**
- Compete directly on same keywords
- Requires superior content + links
- High effort, high reward

**Flank Attack:**
- Target adjacent keywords
- Find gaps in competitor coverage
- Lower competition approach

**Niche Focus:**
- Dominate specific subtopic
- Build topical authority
- Expand from strong base

**Innovation:**
- New content formats
- Interactive tools
- Original research

### Action Planning

Based on analysis, develop:

1. **Quick Wins:**
   - Low-competition gaps to fill
   - Technical fixes competitors haven't made
   - Link opportunities

2. **Medium-Term:**
   - Content gaps requiring significant effort
   - Link building campaigns
   - Technical improvements

3. **Long-Term:**
   - Authority building
   - Brand development
   - Major content investments
