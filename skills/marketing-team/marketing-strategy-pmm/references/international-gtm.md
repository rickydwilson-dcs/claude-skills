# International Go-To-Market Strategy Reference Guide

## Overview
Comprehensive frameworks and best practices for expanding products into international markets, covering localization, market entry strategies, pricing adaptations, and cultural considerations for global product marketing.

## Key Concepts

### Market Selection Framework
**Factors to Consider:**
1. **Market Size:** TAM, SAM, SOM analysis
2. **Competition:** Local vs international players
3. **Regulatory:** Data privacy, compliance requirements
4. **Economic:** GDP, purchasing power, payment methods
5. **Cultural:** Language, business practices, decision-making
6. **Infrastructure:** Internet penetration, tech adoption

**Priority Matrix:**
|--|High Opportunity|Low Opportunity|
|High Ease|Tier 1 (Enter First)|Tier 3 (Later)|
|Low Ease|Tier 2 (Strategic)|Tier 4 (Avoid)|

### Localization vs Globalization
**Localization:** Adapting product for specific market
- Language translation
- Cultural customization
- Local payment methods
- Regional compliance

**Globalization:** Building product that works everywhere
- Unicode support
- Multi-currency
- Timezone handling
- Internationalization (i18n) from day one

## Regional Strategies

### Europe (EMEA)
**Market Characteristics:**
- 44 countries, 24 languages in EU
- Strong data privacy regulations (GDPR)
- Preference for local vendors in some markets
- Longer sales cycles (3-9 months vs 1-3 in US)

**Entry Strategy:**
1. Start with UK (English-speaking, familiar business culture)
2. Expand to DACH (Germany, Austria, Switzerland - largest economy)
3. Add Nordics (high tech adoption, English proficiency)
4. Consider France, Spain, Italy (require localization)

**Localization Priorities:**
- Website: Full translation for DE, FR
- Product UI: English acceptable for tech products, localize for SMB
- Sales materials: Translate key assets
- Support: Local language for Tier 1 markets

**Pricing Considerations:**
- Display prices in EUR
- Consider VAT (19-25% in most EU countries)
- Typical discount: 10-15% vs US pricing (purchasing power)

**Compliance Requirements:**
- GDPR compliance mandatory
- Data residency (some customers require EU-hosted data)
- Cookie consent
- Privacy notices

### Asia-Pacific (APAC)
**Market Characteristics:**
- Diverse: Japan, Australia, Singapore, India, SEA
- High mobile usage (80%+ in some markets)
- Preference for local payment methods
- Relationship-driven sales culture

**Entry Strategy by Subregion:**

**ANZ (Australia/New Zealand):**
- English-speaking, similar to US/UK
- Start here for APAC entry
- Lower compliance barriers

**Singapore:**
- English-speaking business hub
- Gateway to Southeast Asia
- High tech adoption

**Japan:**
- Large market but requires heavy localization
- Relationship-driven, long sales cycles
- Consider local partner or distributor

**India:**
- Large, price-sensitive market
- English acceptable for business
- Mobile-first approach essential

**Localization Priorities:**
- Website: English for ANZ/SG, translate for Japan/China
- Product UI: Japanese essential for Japan market
- Sales: Local sales reps for relationship-building
- Payment: Support local methods (Alipay, WeChat Pay, PayPal)

**Pricing Strategies:**
- Australia: Similar to US pricing (convert USD to AUD)
- Singapore: USD or SGD accepted
- Japan: 20-30% premium vs US (purchasing power)
- India: 30-50% discount vs US (price sensitivity)

### Latin America (LATAM)
**Market Characteristics:**
- Spanish/Portuguese speaking
- Growing tech adoption
- Payment method challenges (low credit card penetration)
- Economic volatility in some markets

**Entry Strategy:**
- Brazil first (largest market, Portuguese)
- Mexico second (proximity to US, Spanish)
- Then: Argentina, Colombia, Chile

**Localization Priorities:**
- Website/Product: Spanish (Mexico, Argentina, Colombia, Chile), Portuguese (Brazil)
- Payment: Local methods (Boleto Banc√°rio in Brazil, OXXO in Mexico)
- Currency: Display in local currency (BRL, MXN)

**Challenges:**
- Currency fluctuations
- Import taxes on software (some countries)
- Payment collection (fraud risk)
