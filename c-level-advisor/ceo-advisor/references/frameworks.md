- Focus: Product-market fit

- Metrics: User growth, engagement
- Timeline: 12-18 months runway

**Series B/C**
- Focus: Growth acceleration
- Use: Scaling operations, team expansion
- Metrics: Revenue growth, unit economics
- Timeline: 18-24 months runway

**Late Stage**
- Focus: Market expansion
- Use: Geographic expansion, M&A
- Metrics: Profitability path, market share
- Timeline: 24+ months runway

**IPO**
- Focus: Public market access
- Use: Brand building, liquidity
- Metrics: Revenue scale, predictability
- Timeline: Sustained operations

**Debt**
- Focus: Non-dilutive growth
- Use: Known ROI projects
- Metrics: Cash flow, debt coverage
- Timeline: Structured repayment

## Go/No-Go Decision Framework

### Major Decisions Requiring Framework

- M&A opportunities
- Market expansion
- Major pivots
- Large investments ($1M+)
- Restructuring
- Leadership changes

### Decision Checklist

- [ ] **Problem Clearly Defined**: Root cause identified, scope bounded
- [ ] **Data/Evidence Gathered**: Quantitative and qualitative, multiple sources
- [ ] **Options Evaluated**: At least 3 alternatives, pros/cons for each
- [ ] **Stakeholders Consulted**: Key voices heard, concerns addressed
- [ ] **Risks Assessed**: Downside scenarios, mitigation plans
- [ ] **Implementation Planned**: Resource requirements, timeline, owners
- [ ] **Success Metrics Defined**: Measurable outcomes, tracking plan
- [ ] **Communication Prepared**: Stakeholder messaging, rollout plan

### Decision Quality Factors

**Strategic Fit (30%)**
- Alignment with vision
- Strategic advantage
- Market positioning
- Competitive impact

**Financial Impact (25%)**
- ROI potential
- Cash flow implications
- Risk-adjusted returns
- Opportunity cost

**Execution Feasibility (25%)**
- Resource availability
- Team capability
- Timeline realism
- Dependency management

**Risk Profile (20%)**
- Downside protection
- Exit options
- Reversibility
- Mitigation strategies

## Crisis Management Playbook

### Crisis Levels

**Level 1 Crisis (Department)**
- Impact: Single team/function
- Response: Monitor situation
- Support: As needed by leader
- Review: Post-incident analysis

**Level 2 Crisis (Company)**
- Impact: Multiple teams/functions
- Response: Activate crisis team
- Support: Direct CEO leadership
- Review: Comprehensive post-mortem

**Level 3 Crisis (Existential)**
- Impact: Company survival at risk
- Response: CEO takes direct control
- Support: Board engagement, all-hands
- Review: External investigation possible

### Crisis Leadership Principles

1. **Communicate Early and Often**: Over-communicate during crisis
2. **Be Transparent**: Admit what you don't know
3. **Show Empathy**: Acknowledge impact on people
4. **Take Responsibility**: Own the situation
5. **Focus on Solutions**: Lead with action plan
6. **Protect Your People**: Shield team from unnecessary stress
7. **Document Everything**: Create clear record
8. **Learn and Improve**: Extract lessons systematically

## Work-Life Integration Framework

### Sustainability Practices

**Protected Family Time**
- No meetings before 8am or after 6pm
- Family dinner priority
- Weekend boundaries
- Vacation planning 12 months ahead

**Exercise Routine**
- 4-5 days per week minimum
- Morning schedule preferred
- Variety for sustainability
- Accountability partner

**Mental Health Support**
- Regular therapy/coaching
- Peer support group
- Stress management practices
- Vacation time taken

### Energy Management

**Know Your Peak Hours**
- Identify highest-energy periods
- Schedule strategic work during peaks
- Protect from interruptions
- Batch similar activities

**Deep Work Blocks**
- 2-3 hour uninterrupted blocks
- Calendar blocking
- Turn off notifications
- Physical location change

**Recovery Practices**
- Daily reflection time
- Weekly planning session
- Monthly strategic thinking day
- Quarterly personal retreat
